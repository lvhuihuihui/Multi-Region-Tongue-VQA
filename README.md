# Multi-Region-Tongue-VQA
Region-aware tongue VQA framework. Official code for IEEE TMM 2026.

Prior-Guided Visual-Semantic Alignment for Structured Tongue Diagnosis and Clinical Reasoning
Abstract
To address the lack of fine-grained partitioning standards and clinical interpretability in existing AI-driven tongue diagnosis, we first construct a high-quality multi-modal dataset comprising 4,535 tongue images. Grounded in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) Zang-Fu partition theory,the dataset follows a standardized annotation protocol. Three senior TCM practitioners conducted double-blind annotations to precisely delineate the spatial boundaries and pathological semantics of five anatomical-functional regions, achieving an inter-annotator agreement of 0.95, thereby establishing a reliable benchmark for fine-grained representation learning. Building upon this, we propose a TCM theory-guided multi-modal fusion framework for intelligent diagnosis. A bidirectional cross-attention mechanism jointly integrates tongue imagery with regional spatial-semantic cues to simultaneously extract global and five region-specific features. Subsequently, a structural graph neural network treats the global feature as a central node and regional features as peripheral nodes, explicitly encoding physiological and pathological priors among Zang-Fu organs to generate high-order fused representations. Aligned with structured clinical labels, these representations are decoded by the Qwen3-6B large language model to perform TCM syndrome identification, nine-constitution classification, and automated generation of structured diagnostic reports. Extensive experiments demonstrate that the proposed method achieves an accuracy of 88%, significantly outperforming instruction-tuned Qwen3-VL and other state-of-the-art multi-modal baselines.
